Overview

A postdoctoral associate at Boston University in Prof. Cheng‚Äôs group, with a background spanning medical science, microbiology, and optical imaging. Work is conducted at the interface of biology, physics, and data analysis, with a strong emphasis on translating advanced technologies into practical biomedical and clinical applications. Particular motivation lies in research efforts where rigorous science can directly improve patient outcomes.

Research

Current research focuses on widefield fluorescent mid-infrared photothermal microscopy for rapid bacterial susceptibility testing. By integrating vibrational spectroscopy, fluorescence imaging, and quantitative analysis, the goal is to develop fast, information-rich diagnostic approaches to address bacterial infections and antimicrobial resistance. Broader interests include microbial diagnostics, applied spectroscopy, and scalable technologies that bridge fundamental research and real-world clinical use.
Ongoing efforts emphasize speed, scalability, and clinical relevance, with the long-term aim of enabling rapid, culture-free diagnostics at the point of care.
